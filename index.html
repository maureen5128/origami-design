Interesting facts about camels

  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Interesting facts about chameleons

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues
  • Chameleons are tree-huggers

Interesting facts about pigeons

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.

Interesting facts about pandas

  • Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm
  • Unlike most other bears, pandas do not hibernate
  • Although pandas eat mostly bamboo and fruit, their teeth are carnivorous, meaning they would be able to chew and consume tough meat

Interesting facts about teddy bears

  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a hunting trip
  • A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project
  • Pooh and Paddington are up there with some of the world’s most famous bears

Interesting facts about cicadas

  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans
  • They can buzz louder than a lawnmower
  • Their wings repel water and bacteria
